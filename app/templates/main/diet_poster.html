Diet Expert System

Prepared by the Expert System Project Team

Department of Computer Studies, College of Science, Norton University

Knowledge-Driven Nutrition
1

Introduction

Nutrition choices shape long-term health, yet most guidance is generic and hard to apply. The Diet Expert System transforms clinical diet knowledge into a personalized, rule-based engine that turns a user profile into clear meal guidance, macro targets, and food selections that feel achievable.

2

Problem Statement

  • One-size-fits-all plans ignore age, BMI, allergies, and dietary restrictions.
  • Clinicians and students spend significant time translating nutrition rules into meals.
  • Users lack a clear list of foods to choose, limit, or avoid each day.
  • Progress tracking and consistency remain fragmented and manual.
3

Project Goal

Deliver a powerful, trustworthy diet planning platform that aligns personal health data, diet type, allergies, and goals into a single, personalized plan with actionable meals and measurable targets.

4

Key Features

Smart Profile Intake

  • Capture age, gender, weight, height, and goals.
  • Record allergies and diet preferences.
  • Choose meal frequency for daily planning.

Rule-Based Matching Engine

  • Expert rules map conditions to diet strategies.
  • Automatic inference selects best-fit plans.
  • Transparent logic supports explainable guidance.

Food Mapping & Avoid List

  • Curated foods aligned to each diet rule.
  • Highlights foods to limit or avoid.
  • Adapts selections based on restrictions.

Metrics & Meal Planning

  • Calculates BMI, calories, and macros.
  • Generates sample daily meal layouts.
  • Supports progress monitoring and updates.
5

Target Users

Health-Conscious Individuals

People managing weight, wellness, or conditions.

Clinics & Dietitians

Consistent guidance with faster plan creation.

Fitness Coaches

Structured meal guidance aligned to goals.

Students & Researchers

A living lab for expert system learning.

6

Technical Approach

  • Curated knowledge base of diet rules and health goals.
  • Inference engine matches user profiles to rules.
  • Food mapping table with nutrition metadata and tags.
  • Safety filters for allergies and diet type constraints.
  • Modular Flask architecture with admin, doctor, and user dashboards.
7

Expected Impact

  • Faster, more consistent diet planning for professionals.
  • Higher adherence through clear daily meal guidance.
  • Greater nutrition awareness and healthier food choices.
  • Scalable foundation for new conditions and diet programs.
8

Conclusion

The Diet Expert System bridges clinical knowledge and daily meals. By combining expert rules with user-specific data, it delivers practical, evidence-informed guidance that is easy to follow and ready to scale.
